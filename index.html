Automated-record data quality

Audit finding: The 48 curated seed records are clean; essentially every identified defect was in the audited set of 288 auto-added records, which made up most of the map. The references were real and mostly well identified, but the auto-classification and de-duplication were not reliable: at least 4 records were off-topic, about 31 were duplicated, and more than 20 were miscategorized. Auto-added records therefore should not be trusted as coded without checking the source.

The pipeline now applies RSV-specific MeSH/relevance checks, excludes abbreviation-only resveratrol records, cross-walks PMID and DOI identifiers before matrix insertion, derives PubMed study design from article types, and quarantines future-dated entries. Auto-added records remain labelled as not manually verified.

      Method and provenance

      This map codes records into a matrix by RSV evidence domain and evidence type. Product, population, outcome, source date, map update date, provenance and tags provide secondary facets.

      Curated seed records are preserved. Automated PubMed records require RSV-specific MeSH validation and use PubMed article types for study-design coding. PMID and DOI identifiers are normalized before de-duplication, abbreviation-only resveratrol hits are excluded, and future-dated records are held outside the matrix in a quarantine log. Auto-added records remain evidence signals rather than verified study summaries. For clinical or policy decisions, inspect the source and relevant official guidance. For research scoping and evidence planning only; not clinical advice.
